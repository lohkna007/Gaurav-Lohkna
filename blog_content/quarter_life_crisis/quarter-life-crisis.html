The Quarter-Life Crisis

Published on November 16, 2024

By Gaurav Lohkna

What Is a Quarter-Life Crisis?

You've heard much about the mid-life crisis and how it affects individuals, but there's a newer concept with a different nuance: the quarter-life crisis. I first used this phrase when describing the ongoing challenges in my life to a friend.

Understanding the Mid-Life Crisis

Let's take a moment to understand the mid-life crisis before moving on to the quarter-life crisis. According to me, a mid-life crisis happens when someone has more or less achieved certain things (think of Maslow's hierarchy of needs), usually involving the basics necessary for day-to-day life—food, clothing, and shelter (or as we say in Hindi, roti, kapda, makaan). Nowadays, the internet feels like a basic necessity too.

How Quarter-Life Crises Manifest

People generally spend about 12-15 years (starting from around age 21-23) working to secure these needs and build a stable life. But once these needs are fulfilled, new challenges arise: What's next? Is it to become more secure? Yes, but that’s an ongoing process, and it will never feel like enough. So, what's next?

The Modern Struggle

In my opinion, what once took a decade to achieve can now be reached in just 3-4 years (at least for those of us going through a quarter-life crisis). We have some securities, we earn sustainable, high-paying salaries (putting us in the top 10% of earners in India), and yet...we feel empty when it comes to fulfilling our personal dreams.
