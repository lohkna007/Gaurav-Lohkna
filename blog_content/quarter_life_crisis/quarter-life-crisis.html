The Quarter-Life Crisis

Published on November 16, 2024

You've heard much about the mid-life crisis and how it affects individuals, but there's a newer concept with a different nuance: the quarter-life crisis. I first used this phrase when describing the ongoing challenges in my life to a friend.

Understanding the Mid-Life Crisis

But let's take a moment to understand the mid-life crisis before moving on to the quarter-life crisis. According to me, a mid-life crisis happens when someone has more or less achieved certain things (think of Maslow's hierarchy of needs), usually involving the basics necessary for day-to-day life—food, clothing, and shelter (or as we say in Hindi, roti, kapda, makaan).

Nowadays, the internet feels like a basic necessity too.

Quarter-Life Crisis: A Different Challenge

In my opinion, what once took a decade to achieve can now be reached in just 3-4 years (at least for those of us going through a quarter-life crisis). We have securities, earn sustainable salaries, and yet feel empty when it comes to fulfilling our personal dreams.

The Questions We Face

This phase of life, I call the quarter-life crisis. Maybe I’m too young to experience all this, or maybe I just want too much. But having a variety of dreams is what makes us human.

How will you decide your life?
